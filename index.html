Conclusion...

The UFC got a lot bigger and has spread around the world

As we can see in the first chart, the UFC has a lot more events nowadays. Also, the events are held everywhere around the world instead of the big fighting countries. This is very good for the sport, because it gives more exposure and entertainment for the fans. It also gives the fighters more opportunities to fight and make money.

The fighters still give badass shows. The knockout rates have hardly changed.

If some people tell you that the fighters back in the day were more aggressive or attacking, then they are simply wrong. At least if you look at knockout ratio's, you see that the numbers change a little bit but even with all these extra events that are held these days, the fighters still manage to maintain (almost) the same knockout ratio's.

The UFC is more professional nowadays

If we take a look at the percentage of matches ending in a disqualification, we see that the number relatively got lower. With all the more matches and money involved, we can conclude that the fighters got more professional in this aspect, as fighting with respect is very important for the sport.
